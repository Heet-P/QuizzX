# QuizzX — Competitor Analysis

**Prepared:** 2026-07-23
**Scope:** Feature, positioning, and pricing comparison of QuizzX against six competitors spanning gamified classroom engagement (Kahoot!, Quizizz/Wayground, Blooket, Mentimeter) and serious assessment/LMS tooling (Google Forms Quizzes, Moodle Quiz).
**Method:** QuizzX's feature list below is grounded directly in the codebase (`prisma/schema.prisma`, `app/api/**`, `components/quiz/**`, `hooks/useQuizCheating.ts`) as of this date, not marketing claims — it reflects what is actually built, not planned. Competitor facts are from live web research (official pages fetched directly where possible, third-party sources cited where official pages blocked automated fetches); every claim below is sourced in [Sources](#sources), and anywhere a fact rests on a secondary source rather than an official page, that's flagged inline.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

QuizzX is a pre-revenue, single-developer student project (per [answer_icreate.md](answer_icreate.md)) that has, in its current state, assembled a feature combination none of the six researched competitors match exactly: **live host-led multiplayer rooms** + **persistent named teams with accumulated scores** + **a persistent XP/achievement/streak progression system** + **active, multi-signal anti-cheat enforcement** (fullscreen lock, tab-switch auto-submit/three-strikes, copy/paste/right-click/print-screen blocking, live proctor dashboard) all in one product. Individually, each of these exists in at least one competitor — but no single competitor combines all four the way QuizzX does. Wayground (formerly Quizizz) is the closest peer on anti-cheat specifically; Blooket is the closest peer on persistent gamification; none of the six have QuizzX's combination of *persistent* teams (most competitors' "teams" are re-formed every session) with a proctor-facing live monitoring dashboard.

That said, QuizzX is early. Every competitor here has years (Kahoot!: founded 2013; Moodle: 2002) of production hardening, proven concurrency at massive scale (Kahoot! markets "billions" of players, Wayground claims 90% of U.S. schools), broader question-type coverage, and — in Wayground's and Google/Moodle's case — materially more mature AI feature surfaces (lesson plans, rubrics, translation, standards alignment) than QuizzX's four-question-type document parser. QuizzX also has no pricing model yet (it isn't a commercial product), no proven real-time infrastructure at scale (Redis fan-out is environment-scaffolded but [not wired up yet](answer_icreate.md)), and — per an over-engineering audit conducted earlier this session — some genuinely dead/orphaned surface area (a comments feature with zero UI, admin routes with zero callers) that signals a product still mid-build rather than shipped.

## 3. Where QuizzX Is Ahead

Ranked by how distinctive the advantage is (most distinctive first):

1. **Persistent teams, not per-session groupings.** All four gamified competitors (Kahoot!, Wayground, Blooket, Mentimeter) that have any team concept re-form teams fresh every game. QuizzX's teams are joined once via a team code and accumulate a score history (`TeamQuizScore.avgScore`, `memberCount`) across quizzes — closer to a guild/cohort model than a round's team assignment.
2. **The anti-cheat + persistent-gamification combination.** Wayground matches QuizzX's anti-cheat feature-for-feature (and its Google-Lens/extension detection is arguably more sophisticated than QuizzX's current signal set), and Blooket matches QuizzX's persistent-progression instinct — but neither combines *both* the way QuizzX does. Kahoot!, Blooket, and Mentimeter have no cheat-detection at all; Google Forms' lockdown only works on school-managed Chromebooks; Moodle's SEB is a heavier, separate-application lockdown rather than in-browser signal detection.
3. **Live proctor dashboard tied to submission event logs.** QuizzX logs `tab_switch`/`copy_attempt`/`fullscreen_exit` events per submission and surfaces them to an admin in real time (SSE). Wayground has a comparable real-time alert dashboard; none of Kahoot!, Blooket, Mentimeter, Google Forms, or core Moodle expose anything equivalent.
4. **Achievement/streak/daily-challenge framing of progression**, vs. Blooket's currency-and-cosmetics framing — a difference in flavor, not in existence, but notable since none of the other five competitors have *any* persistent per-user progression system without a paid add-on (Moodle) or at all (Kahoot!, Wayground, Mentimeter, Google Forms).

## 4. Where QuizzX Is Behind

Ranked by size of the gap (largest first):

1. **Question-type breadth.** QuizzX supports 4 types (MCQ-single, MCQ-multi, fill-blank, match-columns). Wayground supports 15–18+, Moodle 17 natively, Kahoot! ~8, Mentimeter 23 slide types, Google Forms 12. This is the single widest feature gap found.
2. **AI feature surface.** QuizzX's AI authoring is real but narrow — one input format class (.docx/.txt/.md, no PDF/URL/YouTube), four output shapes. Wayground's AI surface (lesson plans, rubrics, worksheets, auto-translation, "why did they miss this" remediation analysis) and Google's Gemini integration (build-from-existing-Drive-files) are both materially broader.
3. **Proven scale.** Kahoot! and Wayground each claim reach in the hundreds of millions to billions of users/sessions. QuizzX's own funding proposal models a 1,000-concurrent/10,000-MAU target and flags that its real-time fan-out (Redis) is environment-scaffolded but not yet wired into working code — the live-room and leaderboard SSE paths are unproven at anything beyond small-scale testing.
4. **No pricing/business model.** Every competitor has a live commercial offering (free tier + paid tiers, several with published enterprise/district pricing). QuizzX has none yet — appropriate for its pre-revenue stage, but a real gap if evaluated as a market entrant today.
5. **Analytics depth and LMS/gradebook integration.** Moodle's item-analysis statistics (facility index, discrimination) and full gradebook integration, and Wayground's/Kahoot!'s district-level aggregated reporting with roster/LMS sync (Canvas, Schoology, Clever), have no equivalent in QuizzX's current per-quiz analytics route.
6. **Product maturity/surface cleanliness.** A same-session over-engineering audit of this codebase found several fully-built-but-zero-caller routes (a comment feature with schema and API but no UI; admin clone/toggle endpoints never wired to a button) — not a competitive feature gap, but a sign of a product still being assembled rather than a finished, pruned surface.
7. **Device-screen flexibility.** Wayground's headline differentiator over Kahoot!-style products is that its Classic mode needs no shared host screen at all (question + answers both render on the player's own device) — useful for remote/large-room play. QuizzX's live room model currently assumes a presenter/display screen is available, closer to Kahoot!'s and Blooket's model.

## 5. Notable Parity

- **Live host-led multiplayer with room codes** — QuizzX, Kahoot!, Wayground, Blooket, and Mentimeter all have this; Google Forms and core Moodle do not.
- **Document-or-prompt-based AI quiz generation** exists in some form in QuizzX, Kahoot!, Wayground, Blooket (via Khanmigo), Mentimeter, and Google Forms — genuinely table-stakes across the gamified segment now, not a differentiator either way.
- **CSV/spreadsheet export of results** — present in QuizzX, Kahoot!, Wayground, Blooket (paid tier), Mentimeter (paid tier), and Google Forms; roughly even.
